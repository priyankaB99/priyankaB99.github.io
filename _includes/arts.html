Music and Dance

Indian Classical Music and Dance is a major part of how I interact with my work and the world.

When I first began learning Bharathanatyam, I never could have predicted that I'd be practicing it still, 14 years later, as a young woman profoundly transformed by every tap of my foot, stretch of my arm, and bend of my torso.

The beauty of music and dance, to me, is its universality and ability to connect humans with each other regardless of background. My incessent commitment to exploring and practicing music and dance constantly pushes me to be a more compassionate, thoughtful human being.

I have trained for over 16 years under Guru Smt. Kripa Baskaran in Milwaukee, WI and am currently pursuing futher training under Kassiyet Adilkhankyzy, a disciple of Smt. Rama Vaidyanathan. I performed my arangetram in June of 2015. I have performed in cultural events and played lead roles in classical dance ballets across Minnesota, Wisconsin, Illinois, and in the Cleveland Tyagaraja Aradhana. In addition to venues in the US, I received the ICCR scholarship in 2016 to perform in the EFCE Programme in Bangalore and also performed in Chennai at the Hamshadwani theatre. In addition to dance, I have learned Carnatic vocal music for over 10 years and continues to perform Indian classical and light music at local venues and online. I have a deep love and dedication for dance and music; with the blessings of my Gurus, I hope to take part in programs where I can present original solo choreography and explore my artistic creativity.
